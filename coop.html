2.1 Problem Discussion

My SABIC affiliate (UNITED) has identified the need to implement a fully comprehensive management system for precious metals in order to maximize the entire value during the life cycle. This involves creating criteria for determining whether to lease and when to purchase, as well as determining how to manage lost leased quantities. Previously, SABIC Corporate managed this work process for its Affiliates, but UNITED would like to assume responsibility for this process going forward. Previously, the lease approach was not accommodated due to the high risk inherited in the international markets in which precious metals have been fluctuating considerably during economic crisis. This fluctuation can affect the operating cost significantly, thus, the management has been discouraged to implement the lease approach even with cost saving it can generate. However, there are certain measure that can help minimize the risk of precious metal fluctuating in the sense that it will be a preferable approach to lease the precious metals in this case the silver. Furthermore, the impact of undertaking the lease approach can reduce the future projects’ cost in which it will reduce the capital expenditures of the company and consequently its profitability as well as the reduction in capital cost will provide the company with additional investment opportunities.

 

The solution will focus on the catalyst equipment in which it will be an integral part of the operation as it is going to be produced in a customized way in the sense the catalyst will include the specific need for the silver in the operation. The project will use the silver as an input during each cycle of the project period of 21 years along with incurring cost in each cycle to replace the lost silver during the operation. The solution suggests that these lost amounts of silver should be leased as it would result in significant decrease in the operating cost. Moreover, to assess the viability of the lease option, a base case is used to demonstrate the current cost to evaluate the resulted reduction under the lease option.

 

 A third part will handle the manufacturing of the catalyst, which is Chevron, to be delivered at the beginning of the 2023 and the catalyst will be manufactured every two years. Other estimation of the cost and expected requirement will be provided in the Excel sheet.

2.2 Solution

2.2.1 Purchase vs Lease

Although usually leasing equipment is considered way cheaper than owning them, there are risks resulted from the inputs in which some inputs are crucial to the operation and the operation process cannot be performed without it. Therefore, it would be much safer to have sufficient amount of the input in this case silver, at the company’s disposal. Moreover, there are few suppliers of silvers and frequent delays might occur that will slow the operations, thus, supplying large amount can be very risky to the company and it have had experienced some shortages in the past.

 

On the other hand, the previous argument was true for decades, but as the technology advanced, it has facilitated the consolidation of the supply chain around the globe. Nowadays, customer can track their shipments from everywhere in the world using software that can notify the company of any expected delays, or the number of cargoes that is expected to be delivered in time. That improvement in the supply chain has reduced the risk of leasing the silver and it can help the company realize a significance reduction in its operating cost. further details of the calculation of the saving cost will be displayed in the appendix.

2.2.5.5 Literature Review

Fear and inflation influence the prices of silver and gold and other components on the commodity market. According to Arendas (2015), unlike gold, silver continues to have a broader range of industrial applications in a variety of countries and industries. As a result, silver is in continual demand, which is tied to the rise of these businesses.

 

According to Arendas (2015), gold and silver often exhibit the same price trend, i.e., an increase or drop, but the amount of the price change varies between the two because the variables influencing them are not same. According to Arendas (2015), silver is more volatile than gold, hence the gold-silver ratio is unstable due to silver's high volatility.

 

According to Arendas (2015), Senoy (2013) concluded after studying the performance of precious metals from 1993 to 2013 that the 2008 financial crises caused high volatility in the case of platinum and palladium, but had little effect on gold and silver, and that the prices of these precious metals began to correlate after 2000. In addition, according to Arendas (2015), numerous academics have determined that precious metals, such as silver, palladium, and platinum, are more stable for exchange rate hedging due to their greater industrial demand.

 

Similarly, precious metals are frequently used to hedge the risk associated with local currencies. Since the US dollar is a single currency, it is used to determine the value of the majority of precious metals. According to Kunkler (2022), precious metals are a frequent method for hedging the risk associated with local currencies, and each of the Great 10 countries uses silver, gold, or platinum to hedge their local currencies.

Using precious metals as a hedge against inflation or stock market crashes is a well-known tendency among investors. Cohen (2022) noted that as the number and rate of the flow of extra financial information increased dramatically, the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in financial asset price forecasting and trading expanded. Numerous sorts of algorithms have been utilized as market experts for applications such as currency and stock markets. According to Cohen (2022), it is possible to foresee the short-term price patterns of all precious metals evaluated. Moreover, Cohen demonstrated that her method estimates price increases more accurately than price decreases for all evaluated techniques and commodities. Cohen said techniques predict future silver prices more accurately than future platinum prices. Linear regression was shown to be the greatest technique for forecasting silver prices using gold as independent variable, whereas the Bollinger band technique was found to be the most suitable for palladium. This indicates that the use of artificial intelligence and its algorithms is still in its infancy, but the findings thus far are promising and can be expanded upon in the future.

2.2.8 Accounting Treatment    

Ø  Own the silver of catalyst and own for silver losses: We capitalize the full silver quantity under the precious metal asset class, which will not be depreciated, and at the end of each year we will re-evaluate the silver amount in today's prices and compare it to the purchase amount; if the purchase amount is greater, we will depreciate the difference.

 

Ø  Own the silver of catalyst and lease the silver losses: We capitalize the full silver quantity under the precious metal asset class, which will not be depreciated, and the losses will be leased as ROU, and we will record a provision for the difference between the leased and owned quantities over time.

 

Ø  Rent PM inside the reactor and bear losses to preserve: Record the total amount under ROU, then estimate the quantities of silver losses each cycle and record a provision based on current prices for these quantities. In addition, we will purchase the amounts lost from this provision. In this instance, the quantity of silver leased will not exceed the initial quantity.

 


Ø  Leasing PM within the reactor and leasing losses: Record the total amount under ROU, then estimate the quantities of silver losses each cycle and record a provision based on current prices for these quantities. And we will continue to lease additional quantities to offset the losses, with the leased quantities for this scenario increasing with each cycle.
